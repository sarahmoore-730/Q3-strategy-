STRATEGY — PROACTIVE MESSAGING

Reach parents before they reach us

Instead of waiting for parents to find their way into chat, we send the first message — meeting them at the right moment with something useful.

WHAT HAPPENED IN Q2

the sleep service team ran the first proactive messaging experiments

The Q2 work was app-side only — proactive messages surfaced on the home screen, outside the chat interface. It was a first signal on whether parents would engage with a proactive prompt at all.

WHAT WE TESTED
Home screen proactive messages
Messages surfaced on the Hatch app home screen, driven by the sleep service team. App-side implementation — not coming through the chat interface itself.
THE LIMITATION
Disconnected from the chat experience
When a parent tapped through to chat, the proactive message they saw on the home screen didn't carry over. The experience felt fragmented rather than cohesive.
WHERE WE'RE GOING IN Q3

Two things we need to figure out

Q3 is about making proactive messaging feel like a real product capability — not a one-off experiment.

01
A true backend injection mechanism
The proactive message should come through the chat interface itself — not just a home screen nudge. When a parent taps into chat, they see the same message that prompted them. The experience feels cohesive: one conversation, not two disconnected surfaces.
02
A scalable trigger framework
Right now, proactive messages are one-off. We need a framework for deciding who gets what message, when — covering more parents across more moments, while making sure we don't inundate anyone who meets multiple triggers at once.
03
Content cards as the first proactive test
Given the strong early engagement signal on content cards, using a content card as the first message format is a natural first test of the injection mechanism. It's something concrete we can ship, measure, and learn from — and it connects two Q3 bets into one cohesive flow.
FUTURE ITERATIONS

Once the infrastructure is in place

The injection mechanism and trigger framework unlock a much larger testing surface. Future work will center on three variables.

WHAT
What we message
Testing different message formats and content types — sleep tips, content cards, NL command prompts — to learn what drives engagement.
WHO
Who we message
Targeting by age, subscription tier, device usage, or behavior patterns to send the most relevant message to the right parent.
WHEN
Timing of the message
Identifying the right moments — onboarding windows, sleep regression age bands, post-purchase — when a proactive message is most likely to land.
