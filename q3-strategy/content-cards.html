STRATEGY — CONTENT CARDS

Surfacing the right content at the right moment

Content cards embed sounds, lights, and stories from the Hatch content library directly in the chat experience — giving users a reason to engage with content they'd never have found on their own.

WHAT'S ALREADY LIVE

In-chat content cards

When Baby Agent answers a question about a Hatch product or feature, it surfaces a content card inline — giving the user a visual, actionable next step rather than a wall of text. Users can preview sounds and colors directly from the chat response, and save them to their favorites.

EARLY SIGNALS

Cards are driving content engagement

Week of Jun 17–23: 95 new users received their first card recommendation. Cumulative: 148 recipients, 3.1% of all-time Baby Agent users. We're less than 2 weeks into content launch — and the numbers are holding up.

WHERE WE MIGHT TAKE IT NEXT · IN PARTNERSHIP WITH MARY

Open directions for Q3

The content card surface is early. These are the directions worth exploring as we scale it.

Proactive first message with a content card
Send a content card as the first message to users who haven't chatted yet — pulling them into chat while surfacing content they'd find valuable.
Drive users to premium content
Surface subscription content — like Lulu — through a card. The user tries it, loves it, and hits the paywall naturally when they want to keep going. Chat becomes a discovery channel for subscription conversion.
Expand across content types
If a user has a sleep sound saved, can we get them into music? A story? A light? Use what we know about their existing preferences to introduce them to content categories they haven't tried yet.
Learn what users do next
After a card — do users come back to chat and ask for different content? Ask us to schedule it? Go play it right away? Understanding that sequence should drive what we build next: more recommendations, scheduling flows, or something else entirely.
